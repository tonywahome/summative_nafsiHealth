Understanding Preventive Health

Preventive health is key to helping you stay healthy and detecting health problems early on, before they cause other issues or become more difficult to treat. It's about routine care you receive to maintain your health and prevent serious diseases before they happen.

Common Preventive Services

Cancer Screenings

Early detection screenings for:

  • Breast cancer
  • Colorectal cancer
  • Cervical cancer
  • Prostate cancer

Metabolic Screenings

Regular checks for:

  • Blood pressure
  • Cholesterol levels
  • Diabetes

Immunizations

Important vaccines for adults:

  • Annual flu shots
  • COVID-19 vaccines
  • Childhood vaccine boosters

Preventive Care for Children

Children's preventive healthcare includes:

  • Regular well-child visits
  • Childhood vaccinations
  • Behavioral and mental health screenings
  • Blood tests for various conditions
  • Growth and development monitoring

Family Health History

Understanding your family health history is crucial for preventive care:

  • Share family history with your healthcare provider
  • Know which conditions run in your family
  • Get appropriate screenings based on family history
  • Take preventive measures for hereditary conditions

Preventive vs. Diagnostic Care

Understanding the difference:

  • Preventive tests: Screenings before symptoms develop
  • Diagnostic tests: Used to investigate existing symptoms
  • Both are important but serve different purposes

Insurance Coverage

Most health insurance plans cover preventive care services with no co-payments, including:

  • Private health insurance
  • Health Insurance Marketplace plans
  • Medicaid and CHIP
